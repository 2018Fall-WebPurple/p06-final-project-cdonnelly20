Biography

Emma González, is 19 year old activist who recently graduated from Majory Stoneman Douglas high school in Parkland, Florida. She is best known her speech against gun violence, "We call B.S." In her speech she called out politicians funded by the NRA including President Trump. She is currently studying at New College of Florida.



Advocacy


"We Call B.S." speech at the Rally to Support Firearm Safety

On February 17, 2018, González gave her 11-minute speech infront of the Broward County Courthouse during the gun control rally in Fort Lauderdale. She delivered this speech only three days after the Stoneman Douglas shooting. In her speech, González called B.S. in response to gun laws. Her speech went viral.

March for Our Lives Speech

González and other students, including fellow Parkland survivors organized and participated in the nationwide March for Our Lives protest on March 24, 2018.González spoke for six minutes, the length of time of the Parkland shooting, and paid tribute to the victims by mentioning each one by name and giving examples of things they would never again be able to do. She then paused for several minutes of silence. González stated in a later interview that people needed to "empathize rather than feel apathy."


Media Appearances

González spoke at an internationally televised town hall hosted by CNN on February 21, 2018. Along with others, she criticized the National Rifle Association as well as politicians who accept money from it. Saying they are being complicit in the shootings, and stated that "you're either funding the killers, or you're standing with the children."González also shares her voice through Twitter. She also was able to share her voice through magazines and newspapers. Glamour Magazine called her "the face of the #NeverAgain movement" and "a recognizable icon." The Washington Post called her "The new face of Florida." González was even featured in 6O Minutes, she described the idea of arming teachers in classrooms with guns as "stupid." She was even featured in the cover of Time Magazine.
